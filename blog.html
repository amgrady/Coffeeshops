Inkwell

Inkwell’s Gingerbread Latte was absolutely delicious; sip after sip it tasted like a perfectly-made gingerbread man cookie. It was very sweet, making it the perfect latte for the Christmas season. They had a small selection of specialty drinks and syrups, but the ones they did have proved to exceptional. Their prices are similar to those of the local coffee shops.

Hopscotch

Hopscotch’s black coffee/cold brews are reasonable priced, and well-flavored; however, when you start to dive into the speciality drinks, the selection lowers and the prices rise. They have a few syrups to choose from, but you won’t find much variety. The price is high for the quantity given. If you like old-fashioned coffee, like americanos and cappuccinos, this is the place for you!

Soma

Soma’s drip coffee/cold brews are flavorful and well-priced. There’s a wide variety of brews to choose from, and the coffee is high quality. The standard coffee drinks are all made well, and they have a large selection of unique specialty drinks as well as several types of loose-leaf teas, freshly-squeezed juices, and fruit smoothies. Whether you like black coffee or caramel macchiatos, Soma has something for you!

Starbucks

Starbucks’ cold brews/black coffee are classic and brewed well. They also have a specialty cold brew with coconut milk, which is a rather unique addition. However, the cold brews do tend to be on the pricier side. The black coffee is reasonably priced, especially if you’re a gold member and can reap the reward of free refills.

Crumble

This drink included honey, caramel, and hazelnut, and it was as delicious as it sounds! This specialty drink was better than expected, and at a reasonable price. It was the perfect mix of both the espresso and the different flavors, while also beautiful presented. This drink was unanimously voted as the best specialty drink.

Needmore Coffee Roasters:

Though this drink was black coffee, and not what we expected, I was really impressed by it. I could taste each individual flavor in the roast, especially the caramel and apple. The presentation of the drink was especially unique because they gave us the coffee in a mug, but the extra was given to us in a chemical flask. We watched them measure everything and prepare it elaborately, which is no doubt part of the reason Needmore had the best black coffee I had in Bloomington.

The Pourhouse:

TThe Pourhouse’s caramel macchiato is mediocre. The caramel and vanilla flavors were just not that great. I was very disappointed by this speciality drink, as I expected better from a place with such a great reputation. Personally, I enjoyed the muffin more than the drink itself.
